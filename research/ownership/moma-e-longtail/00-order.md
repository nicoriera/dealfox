# Moma E-Longtail — Order log

## Context

Nicolas and Sandra bought a **Moma E-Longtail reconditioned** as their first family cargo bike.

The purchase decision happened after comparing several options:

- Fiido T2
- Decathlon R500E Longtail
- Moma E-Longtail
- Gaya
- Jean Fourche
- Yuba Kombi E5
- Cube Longtail Hybrid
- Tern GSD used

## Final choice

**Selected model:** Moma E-Longtail reconditioned  
**Purchase price:** 1,999 EUR  
**Purchase channel:** Official Moma Bikes website  
**Condition:** Reconditioned  
**Use case:** Family mobility, two children, Anglet, partial car replacement

## Why this choice made sense

The Moma became a credible final candidate because of several observed facts:

- reconditioned official offer at 1,999 EUR;
- direct purchase from manufacturer;
- Spanish company with physical store in Barcelona;
- official warranty and technical support;
- 14-day return period;
- official replacement battery available on Moma website;
- battery price observed at 399.99 EUR;
- family-compatible rear rack supporting 2 child seats, 2 cushions, or 1 seat + 1 cushion;
- 80 kg rear rack capacity;
- 180 kg maximum recommended total weight;
- Shimano Altus transmission;
- RST suspension fork;
- hydraulic disc brakes;
- 624 Wh removable battery.

## Remaining risks

The choice is rational but not risk-free.

Open questions:

- Will a local workshop around Anglet accept the bike for regular maintenance?
- How fast is Moma support in real warranty cases?
- Are electronic parts available quickly?
- What is the real long-term resale value?
- What is the real battery range with two children and hills?

## Decision quality

This was not a pure price-based decision.

The decision balanced:

- acquisition price;
- family compatibility;
- official reconditioned status;
- availability of replacement battery;
- acceptable technical components;
- known remaining uncertainty around local support.

## DealFox learning

A reconditioned manufacturer offer can be more interesting than a cheaper unknown second-hand listing because it reduces risk while improving value.

DealFox should distinguish:

- new price;
- manufacturer reconditioned price;
- third-party reconditioned price;
- private second-hand price.
