# Moma E-Longtail — Maintenance and support notes

## Context

After purchasing the Moma E-Longtail, the main concern shifted from choosing the bike to living with it.

The core question is no longer:

> Which bike should we buy?

It is now:

> How do we avoid immobilization and protect the investment?

## Known support signals

Moma communicates the following advantages:

- official warranty;
- direct contact with manufacturer;
- 14-day return period;
- technical advice;
- physical store in Barcelona;
- free delivery on electric bikes;
- official replacement battery available;
- several standard components on the bike.

## Positive maintenance signals

The bike uses several recognizable components:

- Shimano Altus drivetrain;
- RST suspension fork;
- hydraulic disc brakes;
- Kenda tyres;
- aluminium frame and racks.

These are encouraging for regular mechanical maintenance.

## Main risk

The biggest remaining risk is not regular maintenance.

It is immobilization caused by brand-specific parts:

- motor;
- controller;
- display;
- wiring harness;
- battery electronics.

## Local validation needed

The family should ask local workshops near Anglet:

1. Do you service Moma electric cargo bikes?
2. Can you handle regular maintenance: tyres, brakes, drivetrain, wheels?
3. Can you diagnose electrical issues?
4. If Moma sends parts, can you install them?
5. Do you accept e-bikes bought online?

## DealFox learning

DealFox must distinguish two support concepts:

### Manufacturer support

How good the brand is at warranty, parts and technical assistance.

### Local serviceability

How easy it is for the owner to get the bike repaired near home.

A bike can be strong on one dimension and weak on the other.

## Future metric

DealFox should introduce an **Immobilization Risk Index** based on:

- local workshop availability;
- spare parts visibility;
- manufacturer response quality;
- standardization of components;
- electronic dependency;
- expected repair delay.
