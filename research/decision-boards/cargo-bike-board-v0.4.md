# Cargo Bike Decision Board v0.4

## User profile

- Family with two children
- Location: Anglet, France
- Use cases: school runs, groceries, beach trips, leisure
- Goal: partial car replacement
- Budget sensitivity: high
- Maintenance concern: high
- Need: reliable family cargo bike, not only the cheapest option

## Current outcome

The family bought a **Moma E-Longtail reconditioned** at **1,999 EUR**.

This board keeps the comparison open for learning purposes.

## Why Moma won in practice

Moma did not have the highest theoretical score.

It won because the real offer matched the family's constraints:

- official reconditioned offer;
- price at 1,999 EUR;
- direct manufacturer purchase;
- family-compatible rear rack;
- replacement battery available from manufacturer;
- acceptable technical components;
- lower upfront cost than Decathlon and premium brands.

## Remaining assumptions

- Local maintenance is possible but still needs real confirmation.
- Manufacturer support quality is assumed to be acceptable but not yet field-tested.
- Long-term resale value is uncertain.
- Real loaded range is unknown.

## DealFox product learning

The best theoretical product is not always the product that gets purchased.

A purchase decision is made when value, risk, timing and budget become acceptable together.

DealFox should therefore show:

- best technical choice;
- best low-risk choice;
- best value choice;
- best real-world decision for the user's current situation.
